Confirmed International Tournament

Tag Football Asia Cup 2026

J-GREEN Sakai (Sakai City, Osaka, Japan)
Saturday 11 April & Sunday 12 April 2026

Event Overview

The host venue for the Tag Football Asia Cup 2026 has been confirmed as J-GREEN Sakai in Sakai City, Osaka, Japan. This event is an international tournament where representative teams from across Asia will compete in fast-paced, highly tactical matches.

In addition, we will run a Beginner Try-Out Session (for kids and adults) so that more people—regardless of experience—can enjoy and experience Tag Football (8-a-side Tag Rugby).

Beginner Try-Out Session

For anyone who would like to try Tag Football (8-a-side Tag Rugby) for the first time, we will hold an introductory session covering the basic rules and safe play. After the introduction, participants will be divided into teams and play a friendly match, making it easy and enjoyable to learn through gameplay.

About the Sport

With categories ranging from open divisions to age-based divisions, Tag Football is attracting attention as a true “sport for life”—enjoyed by people of all ages and abilities. Because the game uses tagging instead of tackling, there is minimal contact, making it both safe and competitive.

The sport is also highly tactical, featuring kicking options such as the grubber kick, which adds variety and excitement to the game. In Australia—one of the sport’s founding countries—more than 200,000 people play Tag Football, and it is now enjoyed in over 30 countries worldwide.
